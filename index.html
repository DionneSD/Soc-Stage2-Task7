A Tribute to the Almighty Chickpea

A short look at the greatness of chickpeas.

Commonly eaten in countries around the world and well-loved among vegans, the small but mighty chickpea is versatile and boasts a number of health benefits.

A man choked to death after ingesting mashed chickpeas. At first it was thought to be a freak accident but police are now treating it as hummuside...

    A non-exhaustive list of facts about chickpeas:
  • Chickpeas (also known as garbanzo beans or chana) are an excellent source of fibre and high in protein.
  • These little legumes have been found to help prevent diabetes, heart disease and obesity when eaten as part of balanced diet due in part to their low glycemic load.
  • They're super versatile - they can be made into hummus, falafel, roasted as a snack or as a staple in lots of meals (think chickpea curry - yum!)
  • The liquid left over from a tin of chickpeas is called 'aquafaba' and can be used as an egg replacement in lots of baking.
  • They're super cheap so no matter your budget, you can fit in some essential nutrients into your diet. Om nom nom nom nom!

Chickpeas are great, aren't they?! If you want to learn more about chickpeas, check out the Wikipedia page on chickpeas for more information.
